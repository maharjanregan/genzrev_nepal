## Scope

This is an evidence-based report on how technology shaped, enabled, and constrained Nepal’s Gen Z movement.

Technology is treated as **both**:
- an **enabler** (mobilization, narrative reach, coordination), and
- a **control mechanism** (platform bans, network interference, surveillance risk, legal constraints).

## Source standards

We prioritize:
- international reporting with on-the-record claims
- human rights organizations and press freedom monitors
- measurement and network censorship research (e.g., interference testing)
- policy think tanks / academic analysis

We separate:
- **Verified facts** (direct reporting + supporting evidence)
- **Contested claims** (credible but disputed, or lacking independent verification)
- **Speculation / interpretation** (clearly labeled)

## Confidence rating

- **High**: multiple credible sources and/or direct measurement/primary documentation
- **Medium**: credible source but limited corroboration / attribution caveats
- **Low**: plausible but insufficient evidence

## Data gaps (explicit)

- Platform-level metrics (e.g., YouTube watch graphs, algorithmic reach) are hard to verify without API access.
- Attribution for partial internet disruptions may be uncertain unless confirmed by multiple measurement sources or official statements.
