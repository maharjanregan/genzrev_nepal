# Post-revolution: current state

This section summarizes the **current state** after the movement.

**Important:** we will not guess. Where evidence is incomplete, we label uncertainty and list what we need to verify.

## What changed (to be verified with sources)

- Leadership / government changes and their immediate policy signals
- Platform access status (which bans remained, which were lifted, and under what conditions)
- Enforcement patterns (arrests for online speech; protest policing)

## What persisted

- The same platform incentives: virality, influencer amplification, and rumor risk
- State capacity for platform restrictions and legal pressure
- Verification challenges during fast-moving events

## Open questions (data gaps)

- Did platform restrictions meaningfully reduce mobilization, or did they shift channels (VPNs, Viber, Discord)?
- Were any social media regulatory proposals passed/modified after the protests? What is the enforcement record?
- What sustained economic impact exists for creators, SMEs, and telecom revenue patterns post-ban?

## Next evidence we should add

- Official statements (government/NTA) on what is currently blocked vs restored
- Independent measurement confirming platform availability (OONI/other)
- Reporting on post-protest policy changes and civil liberties outcomes

> Next step: once we lock the primary case window (recommended: Sep 2025), we will fill this page with sourced, dated findings and confidence labels.
