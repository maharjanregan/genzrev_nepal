## Summary (draft)

We frame technology as **both an enabler and a control mechanism**. The evidence indicates:

- Platform affordances (short-form video virality, influencer recirculation, messaging coordination) accelerated mobilization and agenda-setting.
- State interventions (platform bans/blocking, regulatory proposals, enforcement) shaped information access and protest dynamics, with measurable disruptions.
- Rapid information flows created a high-risk rumor environment (“infodemic” conditions), requiring fact-checking countermeasures.
- Rights impacts include documented reprisals for online criticism and concerns about surveillance/takedown frameworks.
- Digital disruption produced measurable economic effects (e.g., telecom revenue loss after platform bans) and concentrated costs for youth-heavy creator economies.
